Products

Tools that extend the training.

Two products that complement MECC's training programmes — a content-generation assistant built on our own training library, and a managed compliance poster system for Canadian healthcare institutions.

Flagship product

Training Bot

A retrieval-augmented generation assistant that draws from MECC's curated, SME-validated training library — not the open internet. It generates content grounded in evidence. Every output is cited, scoped, and flagged for human review before it reaches a learner.

What it generates

Scenario-based training exercises

Branching clinical scenarios grounded in your institution's policy documents. The bot drafts; a clinical SME validates. The institution gets exercises that reflect its own obligations, not a generic catalogue.

Performance-support tools

Quick-reference guides, decision aids, checklists, and pocket cards — generated from the same knowledge base that powers the training modules. Formatted for print and mobile.

Facilitator discussion prompts

Case studies and debrief frames for in-service sessions. Tailored to the training area, the role pathway, and the institution's policy context.

Assessment items

Decision-point items mapped to competency frameworks. Not multiple-choice trivia — items that test whether the clinician can apply the obligation on shift.

Plain-language policy summaries

Grade-eight readability summaries of institutional policy for frontline staff. Generated from the source document, not paraphrased from a generic template.

How it works

Curated knowledge base

MECC's in-house training content, SME-validated scenarios, regulatory references, and institutional policy documents. Not the open internet. Not a general-purpose model.

Retrieval-augmented generation

Every response is grounded in retrieved source material from the knowledge base. The model generates from evidence, not from parametric memory. Citations are included in every output.

Human-in-the-loop validation

Every output is reviewed by a named clinical SME before it reaches a learner. The bot accelerates drafting. It does not replace the expert who validates it. No content is published without a named reviewer.

Institutional scoping

Outputs are scoped to the institution's own policy documents and regulatory context. The bot does not generate content outside its validated knowledge base, and it does not store or process patient data.

Guardrails

No generation outside the validated knowledge base
Every output grounded in retrieved source material with citations
Every output reviewed by a named clinical SME before publication
No patient data ingested, stored, or processed
