About

The gap between policy and practice is measured in patient outcomes.

We exist to close it. For the institution. With evidence.

The thesis

A thesis, not a mission.

Clinical capability · Canadian institutional setting

Every Canadian hospital carries a library of compliance policies — accessibility, equity, cultural safety, trauma-informed care, Indigenous health. Those policies describe what the institution commits to. Very few institutions can demonstrate, to a surveyor or a board, what those commitments look like in the hands of the clinician on shift.

MECC closes that gap. We license the training infrastructure that operationalizes written policy into measurable workforce capability — role-indexed, evidence-producing, accreditation-ready. The programme is designed, authored, and maintained by a team with deep experience inside Canadian healthcare education. AI augments the work. It does not do it.

What we bring

Healthcare education experience. Institutional knowledge. Craft.

Instructional design

Scenario-based training authored by instructional designers with healthcare specialization. Every module built for clinical decision-making, not content consumption.

Regulatory expertise

Deep familiarity with Accreditation Canada Qmentum, AODA, OHRC, CNO continuing-competence requirements, TRC Calls to Action, and provincial equity frameworks.

Technical infrastructure

LMS integration, xAPI analytics, PHIPA-compliant data architecture, and Canadian data residency. Built for the compliance requirements of the institutions we serve.

The category we are building

Compliance training infrastructure. A distinction worth the translation.

Not training

Training is an event. Capability is a measured competence, produced continuously, visible institutionally.

Not consulting

Consulting produces a deck. Infrastructure produces a programme that outlasts the consulting engagement — and the consultant.

Not simulation

Simulation rehearses the clinician. Training infrastructure rehearses — and evidences — the institution.

Infrastructure

A licensed, renewable, auditable programme that an institution commits to operate — the way it commits to an electronic health record or a quality-improvement methodology.
