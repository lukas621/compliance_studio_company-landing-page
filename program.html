What the learner walks through.

01

Foundations of Accessibility in Care

The legal, ethical, and clinical obligations that frame every downstream decision. Grounded in AODA, HHS 504, and CNO continuing-competence language.

4 lessons · ~45 min
02

Obligations in the Admitting Conversation

Scenario-based. The first fifteen minutes of patient contact, where accessibility is most often lost and most often measured.

5 scenarios · ~60 min
03

Consent, Capacity, and Accommodation

Authored with a practicing Canadian clinician. The seven decision points that differentiate accommodation from refusal.

7 scenarios · ~80 min
04

Communication Access Across Disability

Cognitive, sensory, motor, psychosocial. A library of role-adapted scripts and debriefs for the conversations that actually occur on shift.

9 scenarios · ~90 min
05

Institutional Evidence & Reflective Practice

The capstone. What the learner contributes to the institution's audit-evidence file, and how reflective practice meets CNO continuing-competence requirements.

3 lessons · ~50 min

Facilitator-led component

Sixty minutes. A kit. A non-clinical L&D coordinator.
