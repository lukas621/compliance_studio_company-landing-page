Three buying scenarios
Scenario I Accreditation runway

"You are fourteen months from your next Accreditation Canada survey."

The written accessibility policy is on the institutional shelf. The surveyor-facing evidence is not yet on the file. You need a program that produces evidence — role-indexed, dated, signed — inside the survey window.

What the program delivers

A Qmentum-formatted capability packet by month four. Role-by-role completion. Clinical SME validation. Ready for the survey letter when it arrives.

Scenario II AODA audit posture

"Your AODA posture was flagged in the last accessibility audit."

The finding is on the record. The leadership team has committed to close it. The instrument that closes it — with measurable workforce capability, not completion rates — does not exist in the L&D catalogue.

What the program delivers

A measured decision-quality delta against the specific IASR training-of-staff obligation flagged in the audit. A closing artefact the Chief Quality Officer signs and files.

Scenario III Board commitment

"Your VP Patient Experience committed to the board. You need evidence."

The commitment was made in a strategic-planning session. The accountability lands in the next board cycle. The institution now needs an instrument that produces something the board can read — in one slide.

What the program delivers

A single-table board readout: obligation, cohort, decision quality, trend. Quarterly, under institutional seal. Un-gameable, reviewable, defensible.

Budget positioning

Not an L&D line item. An accreditation & quality instrument.

The Compliance Studio program is typically funded from Quality Improvement, Patient Experience, or Accreditation Readiness — not from the mandatory-training budget. It is a licensed capability infrastructure, not a catalogue SKU.

Pricing is comparable to a single-quarter consulting engagement, with three-year licensed outcomes. The evidence lives with the institution — permanently.
